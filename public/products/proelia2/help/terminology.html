Help - Terminology

Prepared Encounter
A prepared encounter is essentially an encounter that is being designed. You add participants and regions to an encounter, and then "run" it.
Active Encounter
Active encounters are encounters that are being run. You create them from prepared encounters.
Condition
A condition is any effect, positive or negative, that is applied to a participant. They can be used to adjust a participant's health, or just to track various things that are applied to the participant as part of the encounter.
Group
A group is a pre-made collection of participants that can be added to any encounter. You would normally create a group for your player character groups in one of your game sessions or campaigns.
Participant
A participant is someone who is actively involved in the encounter. Participants can be one of three types: player character, adversary, or object. Player characters are the "good guys", adversaries are the "bad buys", and objects are usually neutral or inanimate participants, such as traps or hazards.
Region
A region is an area of any arbitrary size that can be placed on a map. Regions can have conditions assigned to them that are applied to any participants that are in that area.
Archived Encounters
An archived encounter is an encounter that was run at some point in the past, and then completed. Archived encounters are kept around for reference, but you can also delete them if you don't wish to keep them.
